The food delivery market in India was valued at $8.1 billion 
in 2023 and is growing at 23% annually. Tier-2 cities like 
Ludhiana, Chandigarh, and Jaipur are growing at 31% — faster 
than Tier-1 cities — due to rising smartphone penetration.

Zomato and Swiggy control 90% of the organised market in 
Tier-1 cities. Their presence in Tier-2 cities is limited 
giving local startups a genuine first-mover opportunity.

67% of food delivery startups in India fail within the first 
2 years. Top reasons: insufficient runway below 8 months, 
high rental costs, wrong location choice, and inability to 
compete on discounts with funded players.

Average time to profitability for food startups in India is 
14-18 months. Founders who validated demand before quitting 
their job had 3x higher survival rate than those who quit first.

Minimum recommended runway before quitting a job to start 
a food business is 8 months of personal savings covering 
both business and personal expenses.
